For my BYU Capstone project, I worked with a team of five other graduating engineers. Our team was sponsered by L3 Technologies and tasked with making an autonomous system that could point to a give latitude, longitude, and azimuth with a high degree of accuracy. Military personel often boresight communication antenna. This is a time consuming and potentially hazardous process. L3 hoped to use our research to make the process autonomous and thereby making it safer and more efficient.

Our solution was to develop and rover and base station system that utilized RTK GPS, a Raspberry Pi, and an accelerometer. The rover was set up approximately one-hundred meters from the base unit. The base unit gimbal was lined up with the rover using a scope and leveled using an accelerometer. Once set up, the RTK would establish the position of the base unit. Once location was established, the gimbal would rotate to point to a latitude, longitude, and azimuth that was entered into the Raspberry Pi.
